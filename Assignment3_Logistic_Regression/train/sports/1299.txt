 Serena Williams, aiming to complete the first calendar-year grand slam in 27 years, and Novak Djokovic have been named the top seeds for the US Open starting on Monday in New York. The five-times US Open champion Roger Federer is the No2 seed for the men’s event where the top-10 features five US Open champions and six grand slam winners in all. Andy Murray is seeded No3 with Rafael Nadal No8 and last year’s winner, Marin Cilic, No9 . Williams, the winner of the last three US women’s titles at Flushing Meadows, is followed by the No2 Simona Halep of Romania, a 2014 French Open finalist. Russia’s Maria Sharapova, the 2006 US Open winner, is seeded No3 with the 2014 US Open finalist Caroline Wozniacki of Denmark seeded No4.
